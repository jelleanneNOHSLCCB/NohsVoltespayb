Negros Occidental High School, located in Bacolod City, Negros Occidental, Philippines, is the oldest public secondary school in the province. Its establishment began with educational reforms during the early American colonial period. In 1901, the Philippine Commission passed Act No. 372, empowering provincial boards to fund secondary schools across the Philippines ‚Äî including in Negros Occidental. The foundation for a provincial high school was prepared by Division Superintendent George W. Beattie, who arranged funds and teachers for the first year of operation.

Mid-20th Century Changes
In 1970, RA 5694 converted the school into a national high school under the name Negros Occidental General Comprehensive High School (NOGCHS). This change was opposed by alumni and the community. After more than a decade of legal and legislative efforts, Batas Pambansa Blg. 333 (approved January 19, 1983) restored the name to Negros Occidental High School, the title it holds today.
